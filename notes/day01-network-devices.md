---

# 📖 Network

A **computer network** is a group of connected devices that communicate and share resources such as files, printers, or Internet access. Even two computers connected by a cable form a simple network.

**Example:** Two PCs connected with an Ethernet cable can exchange files.

---

# 📖 Node

A **node** is any device connected to a network that can send, receive, or forward data. Common examples include computers, switches, routers, servers, and printers.

**Example:** Your laptop, Wi-Fi router, and printer are all network nodes.

---

# 📖 Client

A **client** is a device that requests a service or resource from another device on the network. Its primary role is to initiate communication.

**Example:** Your laptop acts as a client when requesting a YouTube video.

---

# 📖 Server

A **server** is a device that provides services or resources to clients. A device can act as a server whenever it shares files, hosts websites, or delivers applications.

**Example:** YouTube's servers send videos to your device when you press Play.

---

# 📖 Client and Server Relationship

The terms **client** and **server** describe roles rather than specific devices. The same computer can be a client in one situation and a server in another.

**Example:** Your laptop is a client while browsing the web, but it becomes a server when sharing files with another computer.

---

# 📖 Switch

A **switch** connects multiple devices within the same **Local Area Network (LAN)** and forwards data between them. It allows devices in the same network to communicate efficiently.

**Example:** All computers in an office usually connect to a switch.

---

# 📖 Router

A **router** connects different networks together and forwards traffic between them. It is commonly used to connect a local network to the Internet.

**Example:** Your home router connects your home network to your Internet Service Provider.

---

# 📖 Firewall

A **firewall** monitors and filters network traffic based on security rules to protect a network from unauthorized access. Modern **Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFWs)** provide additional features such as intrusion prevention and application awareness.

**Example:** A firewall allows legitimate users into the network while blocking suspicious traffic.

---

# 📝 Summary

A **network** is a collection of connected **nodes** that communicate and share resources. A **client** requests services, while a **server** provides them, and the same device can perform either role depending on the situation. A **switch** connects devices within the same LAN, a **router** connects different networks, and a **firewall** protects the network by filtering traffic, with **NGFWs** offering advanced security features.
