# 📖 Main Section

## 🔌 Interfaces and Cables

A network interface (or port) is the physical connection point that allows a device to communicate on a network. Ethernet cables connect devices through these interfaces to transmit data. For example, a PC connects to a switch using an Ethernet cable through its RJ-45 network interface.

## 🔗 RJ-45 Connector

An RJ-45 (Registered Jack-45) connector is the standard connector used with copper Ethernet cables. It contains eight pins that provide the electrical connection between a cable and a network device.

## 🌐 Ethernet

Ethernet is the most widely used LAN technology and defines how devices communicate over wired networks. It standardizes cable types, connectors, transmission speeds, and communication methods.

## 📜 Network Protocols

A network protocol is a set of rules that allows devices to exchange data reliably. For example, Ethernet governs communication within a local network, while IP enables communication between different networks.

## 💾 Bits and Bytes

A bit is the smallest unit of digital information and can be either **0** or **1**, while a byte consists of **8 bits**. Network speeds are measured in bits per second (bps), whereas file sizes are measured in bytes.

## ⚙️ Ethernet Standards

Ethernet standards define characteristics such as transmission speed, cable type, and maximum cable length. Common examples include **10BASE-T**, **100BASE-T**, **1000BASE-T**, and **10GBASE-T**.

## 🔌 UTP Cables

Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) is the most common cable used in Ethernet networks. Its twisted wire pairs reduce electromagnetic interference (EMI) and support cable lengths of up to **100 meters**.

## 🔀 Straight-Through and Crossover Cables

A straight-through cable connects different types of devices, such as a PC to a switch or a router to a switch. A crossover cable connects similar devices, such as two switches or two routers, by crossing the transmit and receive wire pairs.

## 🔄 Auto MDI-X

Auto MDI-X is a feature that automatically detects and adjusts transmit and receive pairs, allowing devices to communicate regardless of whether a straight-through or crossover cable is used.

## 💡 Fiber-Optic Cabling

Fiber-optic cables transmit data as pulses of light instead of electrical signals. They provide higher bandwidth, longer transmission distances, and complete immunity to electromagnetic interference.

## 🔦 Multimode and Single-Mode Fiber

Multimode fiber has a larger core and is designed for shorter distances, while single-mode fiber has a much smaller core that supports significantly longer distances with higher performance.

## 📏 Fiber-Optic Cable Standards

Fiber Ethernet standards specify supported speeds and transmission distances for different fiber types. Examples include **1000BASE-LX**, **10GBASE-SR**, and **10GBASE-LR**.

## ⚖️ UTP vs Fiber-Optic Cabling

UTP cabling is affordable, easy to install, and commonly used for end devices within buildings. Fiber-optic cabling is more expensive but offers higher speeds, longer distances, greater security, and immunity to electromagnetic interference.
